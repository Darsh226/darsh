It is the story of a woman from a small village, named Moti Vavdi, Bhavnagar, Gujarat. She was born with her two hands hanging loose by her sides. She could not do even her daily life chores . But with the support of her parents and family members and above all her own strong will and determination, she is known all over the world as a maverick in Kathiawar embroidery.

Ila Sachani: Embroidering Dreams With Her Feet Summary in English Ila Sachani, from Moti Vavdi village in Bhavnagar, Gujarat, is known for her vibrant embroidery creations. Despite being born with hands that couldn’t be used conventionally, Ila’s mother and grandmother, skilled in Kathiawar embroidery, taught her to embroider using her feet. With determination, Ila mastered threading needles and learned various embroidery styles beyond Gujarat. Initially creating designs for family and friends, Ila’s talent soon gained local admiration and government recognition. Her work was showcased at state exhibitions, leading to national acclaim and awards, including the President’s Medal. Through her art, Ila not only found financial independence but also inspired others to overcome challenges with perseverance and positivity. Her embroidery, crafted with her feet, exemplifies how art transcends physical limitations to touch hearts and souls.
